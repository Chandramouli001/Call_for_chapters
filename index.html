Aim and Scope

The book “TinyML-Enabled IoT Systems: Recent Advancements in Tiny Machine Learning” aims to present the latest research developments at the intersection of Tiny Machine Learning (TinyML) and Internet of Things (IoT). TinyML enables machine learning models to run directly on resource-constrained devices such as microcontrollers and embedded systems, enabling intelligent decision-making at the edge. This book will bring together researchers, engineers, and practitioners to share innovative methods, architectures, algorithms, and applications of TinyML in real-world IoT systems.

Key Topics (but not limited to)

  • Introduction to TinyML: Concepts, Motivation, and Use Cases
  • Edge Intelligence for Resource-Constrained IoT Devices
  • TinyML for Smart Healthcare and Remote Monitoring
  • TinyML in Wearables and Body-Area Networks
  • TinyML for Industrial IoT and Smart Agriculture
  • Neural Network Architectures for Ultra-Low-Power Devices
  • Model Optimization: Quantization, Pruning and Compression
  • Energy Efficient Learning and Inference
  • Deployment Challenges in TinyML Systems
  • Security, Privacy and Trust in TinyML IoT Systems
